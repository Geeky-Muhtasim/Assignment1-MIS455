About Superman

Superman is a fictional superhero created by Jerry Siegel and Joe Shuster, making his debut in Action Comics #1 in 1938. Also known as Clark Kent, he hails from the planet Krypton and possesses incredible powers on Earth, including superhuman strength, speed, flight, and heat vision. As a journalist for the Daily Planet in Metropolis, he conceals his true identity while fighting for justice and protecting humanity from threats like Lex Luthor and General Zod. An iconic symbol of truth, justice, and the American way, Superman has been a cultural symbol for decades, appearing in numerous comics, films, and other media.

Major Events

The Battle in Gotham City

Batman v Superman: Dawn of Justice features a pivotal showdown between Batman and Superman in Gotham City. Driven by his distrust of Superman's unchecked power, Batman dons a mechanized suit to confront the Man of Steel, leading to an epic battle with devastating consequences.

Doomsday's Arrival

The film introduces Doomsday, a genetically engineered Kryptonian monster. After a confrontation between Batman, Superman, and Wonder Woman, the three heroes unite to combat this formidable threat, resulting in a colossal battle that leaves a lasting impact on the DC Extended Universe.

The Sacrifice of Superman

In a climactic showdown with Doomsday, Superman makes a heroic sacrifice to save humanity. He impales Doomsday with a kryptonite spear, but the effort proves fatal. Superman's apparent death marks a significant turning point in the film's narrative, setting up future developments in the DC cinematic universe.
